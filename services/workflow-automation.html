Service 04

Advanced workflow automation

A phone call becomes a transcript, becomes structured action items, becomes tracked work, becomes an invoice — while the team keeps working exactly as they always have.

Start a conversation All ten services

What it is

The work between the work — logging the call, filing the attachment, chasing the approval, raising the invoice — is where the hours go. It is also the work nobody wants, so it gets done late or not at all.

We automate the loop end to end and leave a human at every point where a mistake would cost money. Nothing sends itself. Everything is drafted, queued, and waiting for someone to say yes.

What you get

  • Event-driven pipelines with retries and dead-man’s-switch heartbeats
  • Approval gates wherever an error would be expensive
  • Draft-only by default — nothing leaves without a person
  • Recoverable failures: dead-letter, then replay
  • An audit trail that survives the person who set it up

How it works

4 steps, in order.

  1. 01

    Find the loop

    We watch one full cycle of the thing you do every week and time each step.

  2. 02

    Automate the middle

    The capture and the filing and the extraction come first. They are the least contentious and the highest volume.

  3. 03

    Gate the ends

    Anything that sends, bills, or commits gets a human. Always.

  4. 04

    Instrument it

    If it stops, you find out from a monitor, not from a client.

In practice

Call to invoice, hands-off

In our own operations platform a recorded call is transcribed with speaker labels, extracted twice — once to pull commitments, once to verify each is real — grouped into objectives, bound to the right client, and projected into the work tracker. Time logged against it rolls into a monthly timesheet and out as an invoice document. A person approves before anything is sent.
