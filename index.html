The difference

A data lake tells you what happened. An ontology tells you what it means.

Most companies have years of calls, emails, quotes, and decisions sitting in storage, unqueryable. We build the layer above it: a typed object graph where a person belongs to a company, a call produces a decision, a decision creates work, and that work rolls up into an invoice — so every dollar traces back to the conversation that created it.

  • Entities are minted on first mention with full provenance, never silently collapsed into a catch-all.
  • Near-duplicates are flagged for a human, never auto-merged. We model the uncertainty instead of hiding it.
  • A single audited writer performs every mutation, and each one passes a policy gate that decides run, queue, or suppress.
